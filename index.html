How To Play

Yard Rules

  • Move with `WASD` or the arrow keys.
  • Press `Space` to drop one serving of food.
  • The slab beside the house is the herding zone. Cluster feeding there can trigger a bonus.
  • Fresh food pulls cats in quickly. Old food becomes weaker as it dries and spoils.
  • Fed cats become satisfied for a while and stop crowding you so aggressively.
  • If a cat trips you, half your carried food rolls away and your trip count rises.
  • Go back through the house door when supplies run low to refill to 10 food.
  • Press `P` any time to pause the run.

Scoring

Current Values

  • Feed a cat: `+10`
  • Peacemaker event: `+50`
  • Cat Herder event: `+100`
  • Future TNVR reward target: `+100`

Read The HUD

Quick Reference

  • `LVL` shows current pressure.
  • `CATS` shows how many cats are active in the yard.
  • The food dots show servings still being carried.
  • Ear-tipped cats are already altered.
  • Spray marks come from unaltered male cats marking the yard.

Yard Legend

House

Main anchor of the map. The player starts from the back door and future levels may extend toward the front driveway.

Door

Back door exit where the player enters the yard and brings food out from inside the house.

Shed

Smaller shed landmark where kittens may sometimes be born underneath, encouraging food placement nearby.

Water

Water bowl area that helps define a cat activity cluster without implying a permanent feeder object.

Crate

Placeholder supply or trap staging object that can later become a movement obstacle.

Tree

Large environmental landmark that breaks up open space and creates navigation pressure.

P

Future player start marker just outside the back door for testing house-to-yard movement flow.

Cat Markers

Placeholder cat positions to suggest future gathering lanes and pressure zones.

Ear Tip

Cats with a visible ear-tip marker represent altered cats. Cats without it are currently unaltered.

Spray Mark

Male unaltered cats may briefly stop and mark scenery like the shed or tree, leaving a visible stain marker.

Gate

Yard edge openings that can later connect the backyard to the front driveway or adjacent map spaces.
